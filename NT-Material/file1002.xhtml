Chapter 4

The Parable of the Sower

4:1 Again he began to teach by the lake. Such a large crowd gathered around him that he got into a boat1 on the lake and sat there while2 the whole crowd was on the shore by the lake. 4:2 He taught them many things in parables,3 and in his teaching said to them: 4:3 “Listen! A sower went out to sow.4 4:4 And as he sowed, some seed5 fell along the path, and the birds came and devoured it. 4:5 Other seed fell on rocky ground6 where it did not have much soil. It sprang up at once because the soil was not deep.7 4:6 When the sun came up it was scorched, and because it did not have sufficient root,8 it withered. 4:7 Other seed fell among the thorns,9 and they grew up and choked it,10 and it did not produce grain. 4:8 But11 other seed fell on good soil and produced grain, sprouting and growing; some yielded thirty times as much, some sixty, and some a hundred times.” 4:9 And he said, “Whoever has ears to hear had better listen!”12

The Purpose of Parables

4:10 When he was alone, those around him with the twelve asked him about the parables. 4:11 He said to them, “The secret13 of the kingdom of God14 has been given15 to you. But to those outside, everything is in parables,

4:12 so that although they look they may look but not see,

and although they hear they may hear but not understand,

so they may not repent and be forgiven.”16

4:13 He said to them, “Don’t you understand this parable? Then17 how will you understand any parable? 4:14 The sower sows the word. 4:15 These are the ones on the path where the word is sown: Whenever they hear, immediately Satan18 comes and snatches the word19 that was sown in them. 4:16 These are the ones sown on rocky ground: As soon as they hear the word, they receive it with joy. 4:17 But20 they have no root in themselves and do not endure.21 Then, when trouble or persecution comes because of the word, immediately they fall away. 4:18 Others are the ones sown among thorns: They are those who hear the word, 4:19 but22 worldly cares, the seductiveness of wealth,23 and the desire for other things come in and choke the word,24 and it produces nothing. 4:20 But25 these are the ones sown on good soil: They hear the word and receive it and bear fruit, one thirty times as much, one sixty, and one a hundred.”

The Parable of the Lamp

4:21 He also said to them, “A lamp26 isn’t brought to be put under a basket27 or under a bed, is it? Isn’t it to be placed on a lampstand? 4:22 For nothing is hidden except to be revealed,28 and nothing concealed except to be brought to light. 4:23 If anyone has ears to hear, he had better listen!”29 4:24 And he said to them, “Take care about what you hear. The measure you use will be the measure you receive,30 and more will be added to you. 4:25 For whoever has will be given more, but31 whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken from him.”32

The Parable of the Growing Seed

4:26 He also said, “The kingdom of God33 is like someone who spreads seed on the ground. 4:27 He goes to sleep and gets up, night and day, and the seed sprouts and grows, though he does not know how. 4:28 By itself the soil produces a crop, first the stalk, then the head, then the full grain34 in the head. 4:29 And when the grain is ripe, he sends in the sickle35 because the harvest has come.”36

The Parable of the Mustard Seed

4:30 He also asked, “To what can we compare the kingdom of God,37 or what parable can we use to present it? 4:31 It is like a mustard seed38 that when sown in the ground, even though it is the smallest of all the seeds in the ground— 4:32 when it is sown, it grows up,39 becomes the greatest of all garden plants, and grows large branches so that the wild birds40 can nest in its shade.”41

The Use of Parables

4:33 So42 with many parables like these, he spoke the word to them, as they were able to hear. 4:34 He did not speak to them without a parable. But privately he explained everything to his own disciples.

Stilling of a Storm

4:35 On that day, when evening came, Jesus43 said to his disciples, “Let’s go across to the other side of the lake.”44 4:36 So45 after leaving the crowd, they took him along, just as he was, in the boat,46 and other boats were with him. 4:37 Now47 a great windstorm48 developed and the waves were breaking into the boat, so that the boat was nearly swamped. 4:38 But49 he was in the stern, sleeping on a cushion. They woke him up and said to him, “Teacher, don’t you care that we are about to die?” 4:39 So50 he got up and rebuked51 the wind, and said to the sea,52 “Be quiet! Calm down!” Then53 the wind stopped, and it was dead calm. 4:40 And he said to them, “Why are you cowardly? Do you still not have faith?” 4:41 They were overwhelmed by fear and said to one another, “Who then is this?54 Even the wind and sea obey him!”55
