Mark
Chapter 14

The Plot Against Jesus

14:1 Two days before the Passover and the Feast of Unleavened Bread, the chief priests and the experts in the law1 were trying to find a way2 to arrest Jesus3 by stealth and kill him. 14:2 For they said, “Not during the feast, so there won’t be a riot among the people.”4

Jesus’ Anointing

14:3 Now5 while Jesus6 was in Bethany at the house of Simon the leper,7 reclining at the table,8 a woman came with an alabaster jar9 of costly aromatic oil10 from pure nard. After breaking open the jar, she poured it on his head. 14:4 But some who were present indignantly said to one another, “Why this waste of expensive11 ointment? 14:5 It12 could have been sold for more than 300 silver coins13 and the money14 given to the poor!” So15 they spoke angrily to her. 14:6 But Jesus said, “Leave her alone. Why are you bothering her? She has done a good service for me. 14:7 For you will always have the poor with you, and you can do good for them whenever you want. But you will not always have me!16 14:8 She did what she could. She anointed my body beforehand for burial. 14:9 I tell you the truth,17 wherever the gospel is proclaimed in the whole world, what she has done will also be told in memory of her.”

The Plan to Betray Jesus

14:10 Then18 Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve, went to the chief priests to betray Jesus into their hands.19 14:11 When they heard this, they were delighted20 and promised to give him money.21 So22 Judas23 began looking for an opportunity to betray him.

The Passover

14:12 Now24 on the first day of the feast of25 Unleavened Bread, when the Passover lamb is sacrificed,26 Jesus’27 disciples said to him, “Where do you want us to prepare for you to eat the Passover?”28 14:13 He sent two of his disciples and told them, “Go into the city, and a man carrying a jar29 of water will meet you. Follow him. 14:14 Wherever he enters, tell the owner of the house, ‘The Teacher says, “Where is my guest room where I may eat the Passover with my disciples?”’ 14:15 He will show you a large room upstairs, furnished and ready. Make preparations for us there.” 14:16 So30 the disciples left, went31 into the city, and found things just as he had told them,32 and they prepared the Passover.

14:17 Then,33 when it was evening, he came to the house34 with the twelve. 14:18 While they were at the table35 eating, Jesus said, “I tell you the truth,36 one of you eating with me will betray me.”37 14:19 They were distressed, and one by one said to him, “Surely not I?” 14:20 He said to them, “It is one of the twelve, one who dips his hand38 with me into the bowl.39 14:21 For the Son of Man will go as it is written about him, but woe to that man by whom the Son of Man is betrayed! It would be better for him if he had never been born.”

The Lord’s Supper

14:22 While they were eating, he took bread, and after giving thanks he broke it, gave it to them, and said, “Take it. This is my body.” 14:23 And after taking the cup and giving thanks, he gave it to them, and they all drank from it. 14:24 He said to them, “This is my blood, the blood40 of the covenant,41 that is poured out for many. 14:25 I tell you the truth,42 I will no longer drink of the fruit43 of the vine until that day when I drink it new in the kingdom of God.”44 14:26 After singing a hymn,45 they went out to the Mount of Olives.

The Prediction of Peter’s Denial

14:27 Then46 Jesus said to them, “You will all fall away, for it is written,

‘I will strike the shepherd,

and the sheep will be scattered.’47

14:28 But after I am raised, I will go ahead of you into Galilee.” 14:29 Peter said to him, “Even if they all fall away, I will not!” 14:30 Jesus said to him, “I tell you the truth,48 today—this very night—before a rooster crows twice, you will deny me three times.” 14:31 But Peter49 insisted emphatically,50 “Even if I must die with you, I will never deny you.” And all of them said the same thing.

Gethsemane

14:32 Then51 they went to a place called Gethsemane, and Jesus52 said to his disciples, “Sit here while I pray.” 14:33 He took Peter, James,53 and John with him, and became very troubled and distressed. 14:34 He said to them, “My soul is deeply grieved, even to the point of death. Remain here and stay alert.” 14:35 Going a little farther, he threw himself to the ground and prayed that if it were possible the hour would pass from him. 14:36 He said, “Abba,54 Father, all things are possible for you. Take this cup55 away from me. Yet not what I will, but what you will.” 14:37 Then56 he came and found them sleeping, and said to Peter, “Simon, are you sleeping? Couldn’t you stay awake for one hour? 14:38 Stay awake and pray that you will not fall into temptation. The spirit is willing, but the flesh is weak.” 14:39 He went away again and prayed the same thing. 14:40 When he came again he found them sleeping; they could not keep their eyes open.57 And they did not know what to tell him. 14:41 He came a third time and said to them, “Are you still sleeping and resting?58 Enough of that!59 The hour has come. Look, the Son of Man is betrayed into the hands of sinners. 14:42 Get up, let us go. Look! My betrayer60 is approaching!”

Betrayal and Arrest

14:43 Right away, while Jesus61 was still speaking, Judas, one of the twelve, arrived.62 With him came a crowd armed with swords and clubs, sent by the chief priests and experts in the law63 and elders. 14:44 (Now the betrayer64 had given them a sign, saying, “The one I kiss is the man. Arrest him and lead him away under guard.”)65 14:45 When Judas66 arrived, he went up to Jesus67 immediately and said, “Rabbi!” and kissed68 him. 14:46 Then they took hold of him69 and arrested him. 14:47 One of the bystanders drew his sword and struck the high priest’s slave,70 cutting off his ear. 14:48 Jesus said to them, “Have you come with swords and clubs to arrest me like you would an outlaw?71 14:49 Day after day I was with you, teaching in the temple courts, yet72 you did not arrest me. But this has happened so that73 the scriptures would be fulfilled.” 14:50 Then74 all the disciples75 left him and fled. 14:51 A young man was following him, wearing only a linen cloth. They tried to arrest him, 14:52 but he ran off naked,76 leaving his linen cloth behind.

Condemned by the Sanhedrin

14:53 Then77 they led Jesus to the high priest, and all the chief priests and elders and experts in the law78 came together. 14:54 And Peter had followed him from a distance, up to the high priest’s courtyard. He79 was sitting with the guards80 and warming himself by the fire. 14:55 The chief priests and the whole Sanhedrin were looking for evidence against Jesus so that they could put him to death, but they did not find anything. 14:56 Many gave false testimony against him, but their testimony did not agree. 14:57 Some stood up and gave this false testimony against him:81 14:58 “We heard him say, ‘I will destroy this temple made with hands and in three days build another not made with hands.’” 14:59 Yet even on this point their testimony did not agree. 14:60 Then82 the high priest stood up before them83 and asked Jesus, “Have you no answer? What is this that they are testifying against you?” 14:61 But he was silent and did not answer. Again the high priest questioned him,84 “Are you the Christ,85 the Son of the Blessed One?” 14:62 “I am,” said Jesus, “and you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand86 of the Power87 and coming with the clouds of heaven.”88 14:63 Then the high priest tore his clothes and said, “Why do we still need witnesses? 14:64 You have heard the blasphemy! What is your verdict?”89 They all condemned him as deserving death. 14:65 Then90 some began to spit on him, and to blindfold him, and to strike him with their fists, saying, “Prophesy!” The guards also took him and beat91 him.

Peter’s Denials

14:66 Now92 while Peter was below in the courtyard, one of the high priest’s slave girls93 came by. 14:67 When she saw Peter warming himself, she looked directly at him and said, “You also were with that Nazarene, Jesus.” 14:68 But he denied it:94 “I don’t even understand what you’re talking about!”95 Then96 he went out to the gateway, and a rooster crowed.97 14:69 When the slave girl saw him, she began again to say to the bystanders, “This man is one of them.” 14:70 But he denied it again. A short time later the bystanders again said to Peter, “You must be98 one of them, because you are also a Galilean.” 14:71 Then he began to curse, and he swore with an oath, “I do not know this man you are talking about!” 14:72 Immediately a rooster99 crowed a second time. Then100 Peter remembered what Jesus had said to him: “Before a rooster crows twice, you will deny me three times.” And he broke down and wept.101
