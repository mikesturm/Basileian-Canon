Luke
Chapter 1

Explanatory Preface

1:1 Now1 many have undertaken to compile an account2 of the things3 that have been fulfilled4 among us, 1:2 like the accounts5 passed on6 to us by those who were eyewitnesses and servants of the word7 from the beginning.8 1:3 So9 it seemed good to me as well,10 because I have followed11 all things carefully from the beginning, to write an orderly account12 for you, most excellent Theophilus, 1:4 so that you may know for certain13 the things you were taught.14

Birth Announcement of John the Baptist

1:5 During the reign15 of Herod16 king of Judea, there lived a priest named Zechariah who belonged to17 the priestly division of Abijah,18 and he had a wife named Elizabeth,19 who was a descendant of Aaron.20 1:6 They21 were both righteous in the sight of God, following22 all the commandments and ordinances of the Lord blamelessly.23 1:7 But they did not have a child, because Elizabeth was barren,24 and they were both very old.25

1:8 Now26 while Zechariah27 was serving as priest before God when his division was on duty,28 1:9 he was chosen by lot, according to the custom of the priesthood,29 to enter30 the Holy Place31 of the Lord and burn incense. 1:10 Now32 the whole crowd33 of people were praying34 outside at the hour of the incense offering.35 1:11 An36 angel of the Lord,37 standing on the right side of the altar of incense, appeared38 to him. 1:12 And Zechariah, visibly shaken when he saw the angel,39 was seized with fear.40 1:13 But the angel said to him, “Do not be afraid, Zechariah, for your prayer has been heard,41 and your wife Elizabeth will bear you a son; you42 will name him John.43 1:14 Joy and gladness will come44 to you, and many will rejoice at45 his birth,46 1:15 for he will be great in the sight of47 the Lord. He48 must never drink wine or strong drink, and he will be filled with the Holy Spirit, even before his birth.49 1:16 He50 will turn51 many of the people52 of Israel to the Lord their God. 1:17 And he will go as forerunner before the Lord53 in the spirit and power of Elijah, to turn the hearts of the fathers back to their children and the disobedient to the wisdom of the just,54 to make ready for the Lord a people prepared for him.”

1:18 Zechariah55 said to the angel, “How can I be sure of this?56 For I am an old man, and my wife is old as well.”57 1:19 The58 angel answered him, “I am Gabriel, who stands59 in the presence of God, and I was sent to speak to you and to bring60 you this good news. 1:20 And now,61 because you did not believe my words, which will be fulfilled in their time,62 you will be silent, unable to speak,63 until the day these things take place.”

1:21 Now64 the people were waiting for Zechariah, and they began to wonder65 why he was delayed in the Holy Place.66 1:22 When67 he came out, he was not able to speak to them. They68 realized that he had seen a vision69 in the Holy Place,70 because71 he was making signs to them and remained unable to speak.72 1:23 When his time of service was over,73 he went to his home.

1:24 After some time74 his wife Elizabeth became pregnant,75 and for five months she kept herself in seclusion.76 She said,77 1:25 “This is what78 the Lord has done for me at the time79 when he has been gracious to me,80 to take away my disgrace81 among people.”82

Birth Announcement of Jesus the Messiah

1:26 In the sixth month of Elizabeth’s pregnancy,83 the angel Gabriel84 was sent by85 God to a town of Galilee called Nazareth,86 1:27 to a virgin engaged87 to a man whose name was Joseph, a descendant of David,88 and the virgin’s name was Mary. 1:28 The89 angel90 came91 to her and said, “Greetings, favored one,92 the Lord is with you!”93 1:29 But94 she was greatly troubled95 by his words and began to wonder about the meaning of this greeting.96 1:30 So97 the angel said to her, “Do not be afraid,98 Mary, for you have found favor99 with God! 1:31 Listen:100 You will become pregnant101 and give birth to102 a son, and you will name him103 Jesus.104 1:32 He105 will be great,106 and will be called the Son of the Most High,107 and the Lord God will give him the throne of his father108 David. 1:33 He109 will reign over the house of Jacob110 forever, and his kingdom will never end.” 1:34 Mary111 said to the angel, “How will this be, since I have not been intimate with112 a man?” 1:35 The angel replied,113 “The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow114 you. Therefore the child115 to be born116 will be holy;117 he will be called the Son of God.

1:36 “And look,118 your relative119 Elizabeth has also become pregnant with120 a son in her old age—although she was called barren, she is now in her sixth month!121 1:37 For nothing122 will be impossible with God.” 1:38 So123 Mary said, “Yes,124 I am a servant125 of the Lord; let this happen to me126 according to your word.”127 Then128 the angel departed from her.

Mary and Elizabeth

1:39 In those days129 Mary got up and went hurriedly into the hill country, to a town of Judah,130 1:40 and entered Zechariah’s house and greeted Elizabeth. 1:41 When131 Elizabeth heard Mary’s greeting, the baby leaped132 in her133 womb, and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit.134 1:42 She135 exclaimed with a loud voice,136 “Blessed are you among women,137 and blessed is the child138 in your womb! 1:43 And who am I139 that the mother of my Lord should come and visit me? 1:44 For the instant140 the sound of your greeting reached my ears,141 the baby in my womb leaped for joy.142 1:45 And blessed143 is she who believed that144 what was spoken to her by145 the Lord would be fulfilled.”146

The Birth of John

1:57 Now the time came174 for Elizabeth to have her baby,175 and she gave birth to a son. 1:58 Her176 neighbors and relatives heard that the Lord had shown177 great mercy to her, and they rejoiced178 with her.

1:59 On179 the eighth day180 they came to circumcise the child, and they wanted to name181 him Zechariah after his father. 1:60 But182 his mother replied,183 “No! He must be named184 John.”185 1:61 They186 said to her, “But187 none of your relatives bears this name.”188 1:62 So189 they made signs to the baby’s190 father,191 inquiring what he wanted to name his son.192 1:63 He193 asked for a writing tablet194 and wrote,195 “His name is John.” And they were all amazed.196 1:64 Immediately197 Zechariah’s198 mouth was opened and his tongue199 released,200 and he spoke, blessing God. 1:65 All201 their neighbors were filled with fear, and throughout the entire hill country of Judea all these things were talked about. 1:66 All202 who heard these things203 kept them in their hearts,204 saying, “What then will this child be?”205 For the Lord’s hand206 was indeed with him.
