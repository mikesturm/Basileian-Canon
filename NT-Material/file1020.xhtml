Luke
Chapter 5

The Call of the Disciples

5:1 Now1 Jesus was standing by the Lake of Gennesaret,2 and the crowd was pressing around him3 to hear the word of God. 5:2 He4 saw two boats by the lake, but the fishermen had gotten out of them and were washing their nets. 5:3 He got into5 one of the boats, which was Simon’s, and asked him to put out a little way from the shore. Then6 Jesus7 sat down8 and taught the crowds from the boat.9 5:4 When he had finished speaking, he said to Simon, “Put out into the deep water and lower10 your nets for a catch.” 5:5 Simon11 answered,12 “Master,13 we worked hard all night and caught nothing! But at your word14 I will lower15 the nets.” 5:6 When16 they had done this, they caught so many fish that their nets started to tear.17 5:7 So18 they motioned19 to their partners in the other boat to come and help them. And they came and filled both boats, so that they were about to sink.20 5:8 But when Simon Peter saw it, he fell down at Jesus’ knees, saying, “Go away from me, Lord,21 for I am a sinful man!”22 5:9 For23 Peter24 and all who were with him were astonished25 at the catch of fish that they had taken, 5:10 and so were James and John, Zebedee’s sons, who were Simon’s business partners.26 Then27 Jesus said to Simon, “Do not be afraid; from now on28 you will be catching people!”29 5:11 So30 when they had brought their boats to shore, they left everything and followed31 him.

Healing a Leper

5:12 While32 Jesus33 was in one of the towns,34 a man came35 to him who was covered with36 leprosy.37 When38 he saw Jesus, he bowed down with his face to the ground39 and begged him,40 “Lord, if41 you are willing, you can make me clean.” 5:13 So42 he stretched out his hand and touched43 him, saying, “I am willing. Be clean!” And immediately the leprosy left him. 5:14 Then44 he ordered the man45 to tell no one,46 but commanded him,47 “Go48 and show yourself to a priest, and bring the offering49 for your cleansing, as Moses commanded,50 as a testimony to them.”51 5:15 But the news about him spread even more,52 and large crowds were gathering together to hear him53 and to be healed of their illnesses. 5:16 Yet Jesus himself54 frequently withdrew55 to the wilderness56 and prayed.

Healing and Forgiving a Paralytic

5:17 Now on57 one of those days, while he was teaching, there were Pharisees58 and teachers of the law59 sitting nearby (who had come from every village of Galilee and Judea and from Jerusalem),60 and the power of the Lord was with him61 to heal. 5:18 Just then62 some men showed up, carrying a paralyzed man63 on a stretcher.64 They65 were trying to bring him in and place him before Jesus.66 5:19 But67 since they found68 no way to carry him in because of the crowd, they went up on the roof69 and let him down on the stretcher70 through the roof tiles71 right72 in front of Jesus.73 5:20 When74 Jesus75 saw their76 faith he said, “Friend,77 your sins are forgiven.”78 5:21 Then79 the experts in the law80 and the Pharisees began to think81 to themselves,82 “Who is this man83 who is uttering blasphemies?84 Who can forgive sins but God alone?” 5:22 When Jesus perceived85 their hostile thoughts,86 he said to them,87 “Why are you raising objections88 within yourselves? 5:23 Which is easier,89 to say, ‘Your sins are forgiven,’ or to say, ‘Stand up and walk’? 5:24 But so that you may know90 that the Son of Man91 has authority on earth to forgive sins”—he said to the paralyzed man92 —“I tell you, stand up, take your stretcher93 and go home.”94 5:25 Immediately95 he stood up before them, picked96 up the stretcher97 he had been lying on, and went home, glorifying98 God. 5:26 Then99 astonishment100 seized them all, and they glorified101 God. They were filled with awe,102 saying, “We have seen incredible103 things104 today.”105

The Call of Levi; Eating with Sinners

5:27 After106 this, Jesus107 went out and saw a tax collector108 named Levi109 sitting at the tax booth.110 “Follow me,”111 he said to him. 5:28 And he got up and followed him, leaving everything112 behind.113

5:29 Then114 Levi gave a great banquet115 in his house for Jesus,116 and there was a large crowd of tax collectors and others sitting117 at the table with them. 5:30 But118 the Pharisees119 and their experts in the law120 complained121 to his disciples, saying, “Why do you eat and drink with tax collectors and sinners?”122 5:31 Jesus123 answered them, “Those who are well don’t need a physician, but those who are sick do.124 5:32 I have not come125 to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance.”126

The Superiority of the New

5:33 Then127 they said to him, “John’s128 disciples frequently fast129 and pray,130 and so do the disciples of the Pharisees,131 but yours continue to eat and drink.”132 5:34 So133 Jesus said to them, “You cannot make the wedding guests134 fast while the bridegroom135 is with them, can you?136 5:35 But those days are coming, and when the bridegroom is taken from them,137 at that time138 they will fast.” 5:36 He also told them a parable:139 “No one tears a patch from a new garment and sews140 it on an old garment. If he does, he will have torn141 the new, and the piece from the new will not match the old.142 5:37 And no one pours new wine into old wineskins.143 If he does, the new wine will burst the skins and will be spilled, and the skins will be destroyed. 5:38 Instead new wine must be poured into new wineskins.144 5:39 145 No146 one after drinking old wine wants the new, for he says, ‘The old is good enough.’”147
