Luke
Chapter 9

The Sending of the Twelve Apostles

9:1 After1 Jesus2 called3 the twelve4 together, he gave them power and authority over all demons and to cure5 diseases, 9:2 and he sent6 them out to proclaim7 the kingdom of God8 and to heal the sick.9 9:3 He10 said to them, “Take nothing for your11 journey—no staff,12 no bag,13 no bread, no money, and do not take an extra tunic.14 9:4 Whatever15 house you enter, stay there16 until you leave the area.17 9:5 Wherever18 they do not receive you,19 as you leave that town,20 shake the dust off21 your feet as a testimony against them.” 9:6 Then22 they departed and went throughout23 the villages, proclaiming the good news24 and healing people everywhere.

Herod’s Confusion about Jesus

9:7 Now Herod25 the tetrarch26 heard about everything that was happening, and he was thoroughly perplexed,27 because some people were saying that John28 had been raised from the dead, 9:8 while others were saying that Elijah29 had appeared, and still others that one of the prophets of long ago had risen.30 9:9 Herod said, “I had John31 beheaded, but who is this about whom I hear such things?” So Herod wanted to learn about Jesus.32

The Feeding of the Five Thousand

9:10 When33 the apostles returned,34 they told Jesus35 everything they had done. Then36 he took them with him and they withdrew privately to a town37 called Bethsaida.38 9:11 But when the crowds found out, they followed him. He39 welcomed them, spoke to them about the kingdom of God,40 and cured those who needed healing.41 9:12 Now the day began to draw to a close,42 so43 the twelve came and said to Jesus,44 “Send the crowd away, so they can go into the surrounding villages and countryside and find lodging45 and food, because we are in an isolated place.”46 9:13 But he said to them, “You47 give them something to eat.” They48 replied,49 “We have no more than five loaves and two fish—unless50 we go51 and buy food52 for all these people.” 9:14 (Now about 5,000 men53 were there.)54 Then55 he said to his disciples, “Have56 them sit down in groups of about fifty each.” 9:15 So they did as Jesus directed,57 and the people58 all sat down.

9:16 Then59 he took the five loaves and the two fish, and looking up to heaven he gave thanks60 and broke them. He gave them to the disciples to set before the crowd. 9:17 They all ate and were satisfied, and what was left over61 was picked up—twelve baskets of broken pieces.

Peter’s Confession

9:18 Once62 when Jesus63 was praying64 by himself, and his disciples were nearby, he asked them,65 “Who do the crowds say that I am?”66 9:19 They67 answered,68 “John the Baptist; others say Elijah;69 and still others that one of the prophets of long ago has risen.”70 9:20 Then71 he said to them, “But who do you say that I am?” Peter72 answered,73 “The Christ74 of God.” 9:21 But he forcefully commanded75 them not to tell this to anyone,76 9:22 saying, “The Son of Man must suffer77 many things and be rejected by the elders,78 chief priests, and experts in the law,79 and be killed, and on the third day be raised.”80

A Call to Discipleship

9:23 Then81 he said to them all,82 “If anyone wants to become my follower,83 he must deny84 himself, take up his cross daily,85 and follow me. 9:24 For whoever wants to save his life86 will lose it,87 but whoever loses his life because of me88 will save it. 9:25 For what does it benefit a person89 if he gains the whole world but loses or forfeits himself? 9:26 For whoever is ashamed90 of me and my words, the Son of Man will be ashamed of that person91 when he comes in his glory and in the glory92 of the Father and of the holy angels. 9:27 But I tell you most certainly,93 there are some standing here who will not94 experience95 death before they see the kingdom of God.”96

The Transfiguration

9:28 Now97 about eight days98 after these sayings, Jesus99 took with him Peter, John, and James, and went up the mountain to pray. 9:29 As100 he was praying,101 the appearance of his face was transformed,102 and his clothes became very bright, a brilliant white.103 9:30 Then104 two men, Moses and Elijah,105 began talking with him.106 9:31 They appeared in glorious splendor and spoke about his departure107 that he was about to carry out108 at Jerusalem. 9:32 Now Peter and those with him were quite sleepy,110 but as they became fully awake,111 they saw his glory and the two men standing with him. 9:33 Then112 as the men113 were starting to leave,114 Peter said to Jesus, “Master, it is good for us to be here. Let us make three shelters,115 one for you and one for Moses and one for Elijah”—not knowing what he was saying. 9:34 As116 he was saying this, a cloud117 came118 and overshadowed119 them, and they were afraid as they entered the cloud. 9:35 Then120 a voice came from the cloud, saying, “This is my Son, my Chosen One.121 Listen to him!”122 9:36 After123 the voice had spoken, Jesus was found alone. So124 they kept silent and told no one125 at that time126 anything of what they had seen.

Healing a Boy with an Unclean Spirit

9:37 Now on127 the next day, when they had come down from the mountain, a large crowd met him. 9:38 Then128 a man from the crowd cried out,129 “Teacher, I beg you to look at130 my son—he is my only child! 9:39 A131 spirit seizes him, and he suddenly screams;132 it throws him into convulsions133 and causes him to foam at the mouth. It hardly ever leaves him alone, torturing134 him severely. 9:40 I135 begged136 your disciples to cast it out, but137 they could not do so.”138 9:41 Jesus answered,139 “You140 unbelieving141 and perverse generation! How much longer142 must I be with you and endure143 you?144 Bring your son here.” 9:42 As145 the boy146 was approaching, the demon threw him to the ground147 and shook him with convulsions.148 But Jesus rebuked149 the unclean150 spirit, healed the boy, and gave him back to his father. 9:43 Then151 they were all astonished at the mighty power152 of God.

Another Prediction of Jesus’ Suffering

But while the entire crowd153 was amazed at everything Jesus154 was doing, he said to his disciples, 9:44 “Take these words to heart,155 for the Son of Man is going to be betrayed into the hands of men.”156 9:45 But they did not understand this statement; its meaning157 had been concealed158 from them, so that they could not grasp it. Yet159 they were afraid to ask him about this statement.

Concerning the Greatest

9:46 Now an argument started among the disciples160 as to which of them might be161 the greatest. 9:47 But when Jesus discerned their innermost thoughts,162 he took a child, had him stand by163 his side, 9:48 and said to them, “Whoever welcomes164 this child165 in my name welcomes me, and whoever welcomes me welcomes the one who sent me, for the one who is least among you all is the one who is great.”166

On the Right Side

9:49 John answered,167 “Master, we saw someone casting out demons in your name, and we tried to stop168 him because he is not a disciple169 along with us.” 9:50 But Jesus said to him, “Do not stop him, for whoever is not against you is for you.”

Rejection in Samaria

9:51 Now when170 the days drew near171 for him to be taken up,172 Jesus173 set out resolutely174 to go to Jerusalem. 9:52 He176 sent messengers on ahead of him.177 As they went along,178 they entered a Samaritan village to make things ready in advance179 for him, 9:53 but the villagers180 refused to welcome181 him, because he was determined to go to Jerusalem.182 9:54 Now when his disciples James and John saw this, they said, “Lord, do you want us to call fire to come down from heaven and consume183 them?”184 9:55 But Jesus185 turned and rebuked them,186 9:56 and they went on to another village.

Challenging Professed Followers

9:57 As187 they were walking188 along the road, someone said to him, “I will follow you wherever you go.”189 9:58 Jesus said to him, “Foxes have dens and the birds in the sky190 have nests, but the Son of Man has no place to lay his head.”191 9:59 Jesus192 said to another, “Follow me.” But he replied,193 “Lord, first let me go and bury my father.” 9:60 But Jesus194 said to him, “Let the dead bury their own dead,195 but as for you, go and proclaim the kingdom of God.”196 9:61 Yet197 another said, “I will follow you, Lord, but first let me say goodbye to my family.”198 9:62 Jesus199 said to him, “No one who puts his200 hand to the plow and looks back201 is fit for the kingdom of God.”202
