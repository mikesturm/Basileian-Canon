Luke
Chapter 10

The Mission of the Seventy-Two

10:1 After this1 the Lord appointed seventy-two2 others and sent them on ahead of him two by two into every town3 and place where he himself was about to go. 10:2 He4 said to them, “The harvest is plentiful, but the workers are few. Therefore ask the Lord of the harvest5 to send out6 workers into his harvest. 10:3 Go! I7 am sending you out like lambs8 surrounded by wolves.9 10:4 Do not carry10 a money bag,11 a traveler’s bag,12 or sandals, and greet no one on the road.13 10:5 Whenever14 you enter a house,15 first say, ‘May peace16 be on this house!’ 10:6 And if a peace-loving person17 is there, your peace will remain on him, but if not, it will return to you.18 10:7 Stay19 in that same house, eating and drinking what they give you,20 for the worker deserves his pay.21 Do not move around from house to house. 10:8 Whenever22 you enter a town23 and the people24 welcome you, eat what is set before you. 10:9 Heal25 the sick in that town26 and say to them, ‘The kingdom of God27 has come upon28 you!’ 10:10 But whenever29 you enter a town30 and the people31 do not welcome32 you, go into its streets33 and say, 10:11 ‘Even the dust of your town34 that clings to our feet we wipe off35 against you.36 Nevertheless know this: The kingdom of God has come.’37 10:12 I tell you, it will be more bearable on that day for Sodom38 than for that town!39

10:13 “Woe to you, Chorazin!40 Woe to you, Bethsaida! For if41 the miracles42 done in you had been done in Tyre and Sidon,44 they would have repented long ago, sitting in sackcloth and ashes.45 10:14 But it will be more bearable for Tyre and Sidon46 in the judgment than for you! 10:15 And you, Capernaum,47 will you be exalted to heaven?48 No, you will be thrown down to Hades!49

10:16 “The one who listens50 to you listens to me,51 and the one who rejects you rejects me, and the one who rejects me rejects52 the one who sent me.”53

10:17 Then54 the seventy-two55 returned with joy, saying, “Lord, even the demons submit to56 us in your name!”57 10:18 So58 he said to them, “I saw59 Satan fall60 like lightning61 from heaven. 10:19 Look, I have given you authority to tread62 on snakes and scorpions63 and on the full force of the enemy,64 and nothing will65 hurt you. 10:20 Nevertheless, do not rejoice that66 the spirits submit to you, but rejoice67 that your names stand written68 in heaven.”

10:21 On that same occasion69 Jesus70 rejoiced71 in the Holy Spirit and said, “I praise72 you, Father, Lord73 of heaven and earth, because74 you have hidden these things from the wise75 and intelligent, and revealed them to little children.76 Yes, Father, for this was your gracious will.77 10:22 All things have been given to me by my Father.78 No one knows who the Son is except the Father, or who the Father is except the Son and anyone to whom the Son decides79 to reveal him.”

10:23 Then80 Jesus81 turned82 to his83 disciples and said privately, “Blessed84 are the eyes that see what you see! 10:24 For I tell you that many prophets and kings longed to see85 what you see but did not see it, and to hear what you hear but did not hear it.”

The Parable of the Good Samaritan

10:25 Now86 an expert in religious law87 stood up to test Jesus,88 saying, “Teacher, what must I do to inherit eternal life?”89 10:26 He said to him, “What is written in the law? How do you understand it?”90 10:27 The expert91 answered, “Love92 the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, with all your strength, and with all your mind,93 and love your neighbor as yourself.”94 10:28 Jesus95 said to him, “You have answered correctly;96 do this, and you will live.”

10:29 But the expert,97 wanting to justify98 himself, said to Jesus, “And who is my neighbor?” 10:30 Jesus replied,99 “A man was going down100 from Jerusalem to Jericho, and fell into the hands of robbers, who stripped him, beat103 him up, and went off, leaving him half dead.104 10:31 Now by chance105 a priest was going down that road, but106 when he saw the injured man107 he passed by108 on the other side.109 10:32 So too a Levite, when he came up to110 the place and saw him,111 passed by on the other side. 10:33 But112 a Samaritan113 who was traveling114 came to where the injured man115 was, and when he saw him, he felt compassion for him.116 10:34 He117 went up to him118 and bandaged his wounds, pouring olive oil119 and wine on them. Then120 he put him on121 his own animal,122 brought him to an inn, and took care of him. 10:35 The123 next day he took out two silver coins124 and gave them to the innkeeper, saying, ‘Take care of him, and whatever else you spend, I will repay you when I come back this way.’125 10:36 Which of these three do you think became a neighbor126 to the man who fell into the hands of the robbers?” 10:37 The expert in religious law127 said, “The one who showed mercy128 to him.” So129 Jesus said to him, “Go and do130 the same.”

Jesus and Martha

Tell141 her to help me.” 10:41 But the Lord142 answered her,143 “Martha, Martha,144 you are worried and troubled145 about many things, 10:42 but one thing146 is needed. Mary has chosen the best147 part; it will not be taken away from her.”
