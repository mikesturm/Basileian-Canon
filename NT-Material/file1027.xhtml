Luke
Chapter 12

Fear God, Not People

12:1 Meanwhile,1 when many thousands of the crowd had gathered so that they were trampling on one another, Jesus2 began to speak first to his disciples, “Be on your guard against3 the yeast of the Pharisees,4 which is hypocrisy.5 12:2 Nothing is hidden6 that will not be revealed,7 and nothing is secret that will not be made known. 12:3 So then8 whatever you have said in the dark will be heard in the light, and what you have whispered9 in private rooms10 will be proclaimed from the housetops.11

12:4 “I12 tell you, my friends, do not be afraid of those who kill the body,13 and after that have nothing more they can do. 12:5 But I will warn14 you whom you should fear: Fear the one who, after the killing,15 has authority to throw you16 into hell.17 Yes, I tell you, fear him! 12:6 Aren’t five sparrows sold for two pennies?18 Yet not one of them is forgotten before God.19 12:7 In fact, even the hairs on your head are all numbered. Do not be afraid;20 you are more valuable than many sparrows.

12:8 “I21 tell you, whoever acknowledges22 me before men,23 the Son of Man will also acknowledge24 before God’s angels. 12:9 But the one who denies me before men will be denied before God’s angels. 12:10 And everyone who speaks a word against the Son of Man will be forgiven, but the person who blasphemes against the Holy Spirit25 will not be forgiven.26 12:11 But when they bring you before the synagogues,27 the28 rulers, and the authorities, do not worry about how you should make your defense29 or what you should say, 12:12 for the Holy Spirit will teach you at that moment30 what you must say.”31

The Parable of the Rich Landowner

12:13 Then32 someone from the crowd said to him, “Teacher, tell33 my brother to divide the inheritance with me.” 12:14 But Jesus34 said to him, “Man,35 who made me a judge or arbitrator between you two?”36 12:15 Then37 he said to them, “Watch out and guard yourself from38 all types of greed,39 because one’s life does not consist in the abundance of his possessions.” 12:16 He then40 told them a parable:41 “The land of a certain rich man produced42 an abundant crop, 12:17 so43 he thought to himself,44 ‘What should I do, for I have nowhere to store my crops?’45 12:18 Then46 he said, ‘I47 will do this: I will tear down my barns and build bigger ones, and there I will store all my grain and my goods. 12:19 And I will say to myself,48 “You have plenty of goods stored up for many years; relax, eat, drink, celebrate!”’ 12:20 But God said to him, ‘You fool! This very night your life49 will be demanded back from50 you, but who will get what you have prepared for yourself?’51 12:21 So it is with the one who stores up riches for himself,52 but is not rich toward God.”

Exhortation Not to Worry

12:22 Then53 Jesus54 said to his55 disciples, “Therefore I tell you, do not worry56 about your57 life, what you will eat, or about your58 body, what you will wear. 12:23 For there is more to life than food, and more to the body than clothing. 12:24 Consider the ravens:59 They do not sow or reap, they have no storeroom or barn, yet God feeds60 them. How much more valuable are you than the birds! 12:25 And which of you by worrying61 can add an hour to his life?62 12:26 So if63 you cannot do such a very little thing as this, why do you worry about64 the rest? 12:27 Consider how the flowers65 grow; they do not work66 or spin. Yet I tell you, not even Solomon in all his glory was clothed like one of these! 12:28 And if67 this is how God clothes the wild grass,68 which is here69 today and tomorrow is tossed into the fire to heat the oven,70 how much more71 will he clothe you, you people of little faith! 12:29 So72 do not be overly concerned about73 what you will eat and what you will drink, and do not worry about such things.74 12:30 For all the nations of the world pursue75 these things, and your Father knows that you need them. 12:31 Instead, pursue76 his77 kingdom,78 and these things will be given to you as well.

12:32 “Do not be afraid, little flock, for your Father is well pleased79 to give you the kingdom. 12:33 Sell your possessions80 and give to the poor.81 Provide yourselves purses that do not wear out—a treasure in heaven82 that never decreases,83 where no thief approaches and no moth84 destroys. 12:34 For where your treasure85 is, there your heart will be also.

Call to Faithful Stewardship

12:35 “Get dressed for service86 and keep your lamps burning;87 12:36 be like people88 waiting for their master to come back from the wedding celebration,89 so that when he comes and knocks they can immediately open the door for him. 12:37 Blessed are those slaves90 whom their master finds alert91 when he returns! I tell you the truth,92 he will dress himself to serve,93 have them take their place at the table,94 and will come95 and wait on them!96 12:38 Even if he comes in the second or third watch of the night97 and finds them alert,98 blessed are those slaves!99 12:39 But understand this: If the owner of the house had known at what hour the thief100 was coming, he would not have let101 his house be broken into. 12:40 You also must be ready, because the Son of Man will come at an hour when you do not expect him.”102

12:41 Then103 Peter said, “Lord, are you telling this parable for us or for everyone?”104 12:42 The Lord replied,105 “Who then is the faithful and wise manager,106 whom the master puts in charge of his household servants,107 to give them their allowance of food at the proper time? 12:43 Blessed is that slave108 whom his master finds at work109 when he returns. 12:44 I tell you the truth,110 the master111 will put him in charge of all his possessions. 12:45 But if112 that113 slave should say to himself,114 ‘My master is delayed115 in returning,’ and he begins to beat116 the other117 slaves, both men and women,118 and to eat, drink, and get drunk, 12:46 then the master of that slave will come on a day when he does not expect him and at an hour he does not foresee, and will cut him in two,119 and assign him a place with the unfaithful.120 12:47 That121 servant who knew his master’s will but did not get ready or do what his master asked122 will receive a severe beating. 12:48 But the one who did not know his master’s will123 and did things worthy of punishment124 will receive a light beating.125 From everyone who has been given much, much will be required,126 and from the one who has been entrusted with much,127 even more will be asked.128

Not Peace, but Division

12:49 “I have come129 to bring130 fire on the earth—and how I wish it were already kindled! 12:50 I have a baptism131 to undergo,132 and how distressed I am until it is finished! 12:51 Do you think I have come to bring peace on earth? No, I tell you, but rather division!133 12:52 For from now on134 there will be five in one household divided, three against two and two against three. 12:53 They will be divided,135 father against son and son against father, mother against daughter and daughter against mother, mother-in-law against her daughter-in-law and daughter-in-law against mother-in-law.”

Reading the Signs

12:54 Jesus136 also said to the crowds, “When you see a cloud rising in the west,137 you say at once, ‘A rainstorm138 is coming,’ and it does. 12:55 And when you see the south wind139 blowing, you say, ‘There will be scorching heat,’ and there is. 12:56 You hypocrites!140 You know how to interpret the appearance of the earth and the sky, but how can you not know how141 to interpret the present time?

Clear the Debts

12:57 “And142 why don’t you judge for yourselves what is right? 12:58 As you are going with your accuser before the magistrate,143 make an effort to settle with him on the way, so that he will not drag you before the judge, and the judge hand you over to the officer,144 and the officer throw you into prison. 12:59 I tell you, you will never get out of there until you have paid the very last cent!”145
