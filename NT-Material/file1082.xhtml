Acts
Chapter 20

Paul Travels Through Macedonia and Greece

20:1 After the disturbance had ended, Paul sent for the disciples, and after encouraging1 them and saying farewell,2 he left to go to Macedonia.3 20:2 After he had gone through those regions4 and spoken many words of encouragement5 to the believers there,6 he came to Greece,7 20:3 where he stayed8 for three months. Because the Jews had made9 a plot10 against him as he was intending11 to sail12 for Syria, he decided13 to return through Macedonia.14 20:4 Paul15 was accompanied by Sopater son of Pyrrhus from Berea,16 Aristarchus and Secundus from Thessalonica,17 Gaius18 from Derbe,19 and Timothy, as well as Tychicus and Trophimus from the province of Asia.20 20:5 These had gone on ahead21 and were waiting for us22 in Troas.23 20:6 We24 sailed away from Philippi after the days of Unleavened Bread,26 and within five days27 we came to the others28 in Troas,29 where we stayed for seven days. 20:7 On the first day30 of the week, when we met31 to break bread, Paul began to speak32 to the people, and because he intended33 to leave the next day, he extended34 his message until midnight. 20:8 (Now there were many lamps35 in the upstairs room where we were meeting.)36 20:9 A young man named Eutychus, who was sitting in the window,37 was sinking38 into a deep sleep while Paul continued to speak39 for a long time. Fast asleep,40 he fell down from the third story and was picked up dead. 20:10 But Paul went down,41 threw himself42 on the young man,43 put his arms around him,44 and said, “Do not be distressed, for he is still alive!”45 20:11 Then Paul46 went back upstairs,47 and after he had broken bread and eaten, he talked with them48 a long time, until dawn. Then he left. 20:12 They took the boy home alive and were greatly49 comforted.

The Voyage to Miletus

20:13 We went on ahead50 to the ship and put out to sea51 for Assos,52 intending53 to take Paul aboard there, for he had arranged it this way.54 He55 himself was intending56 to go there by land.57 20:14 When he met us in Assos,58 we took him aboard59 and went to Mitylene.60 20:15 We set sail61 from there, and on the following day we arrived off Chios.62 The next day we approached63 Samos,64 and the day after that we arrived at Miletus.65 20:16 For Paul had decided to sail past Ephesus so as not to spend time67 in the province of Asia,68 for he was hurrying69 to arrive in Jerusalem, if possible,71 by the day of Pentecost. 20:17 From Miletus72 he sent a message73 to Ephesus, telling the elders of the church to come to him.74

20:18 When they arrived, he said to them, “You yourselves know how I lived75 the whole time I was with you, from the first day I set foot76 in the province of Asia,77 20:19 serving the Lord with all humility78 and with tears, and with the trials that happened to me because of the plots79 of the Jews. 20:20 You know that I did not hold back from proclaiming80 to you anything that would be helpful,81 and from teaching you publicly82 and from house to house, 20:21 testifying83 to both Jews and Greeks about repentance toward God and faith in our Lord Jesus.84 20:22 And now,85 compelled86 by the Spirit, I am going to Jerusalem87 without knowing what will happen to me there,88 20:23 except89 that the Holy Spirit warns90 me in town after town91 that92 imprisonment93 and persecutions94 are waiting for me. 20:24 But I do not consider my life95 worth anything96 to myself, so that97 I may finish my task98 and the ministry that I received from the Lord Jesus, to testify to the good news99 of God’s grace.

20:25 “And now100 I know that none101 of you among whom I went around proclaiming the kingdom102 will see me103 again. 20:26 Therefore I declare104 to you today that I am innocent105 of the blood of you all.106 20:27 For I did not hold back from107 announcing108 to you the whole purpose109 of God. 20:28 Watch out for110 yourselves and for all the flock of which111 the Holy Spirit has made you overseers,112 to shepherd the church of God113 that he obtained114 with the blood of his own Son.115 20:29 I know that after I am gone116 fierce wolves117 will come in among you, not sparing the flock. 20:30 Even from among your own group118 men119 will arise, teaching perversions of the truth120 to draw the disciples away after them. 20:31 Therefore be alert,121 remembering that night and day for three years I did not stop warning122 each one of you with tears. 20:32 And now I entrust123 you to God and to the message124 of his grace. This message125 is able to build you up and give you an inheritance among all those who are sanctified. 20:33 I have desired126 no one’s silver or gold or clothing. 20:34 You yourselves know that these hands of mine127 provided for my needs and the needs of those who were with me. 20:35 By all these things,128 I have shown you that by working in this way we must help129 the weak,130 and remember the words of the Lord Jesus that he himself said, ‘It is more blessed to give than to receive.’”131

20:36 When132 he had said these things, he knelt down133 with them all and prayed. 20:37 They all began to weep loudly,134 and hugged135 Paul and kissed him,136 20:38 especially saddened137 by what138 he had said, that they were not going to see him139 again. Then they accompanied140 him to the ship.
